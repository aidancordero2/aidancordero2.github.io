Research Areas

Massively Parallel Reporter Assays

Our experimental work uses massively parallel reporter assays (MPRAs) to measure the effects that variant gene regulatory sequences have on gene expression. We pursue MPRA experiments in two biological contexts: alternative mRNA splicing in human cells and transcriptional regulation in bacteria.

Alternative mRNA Splicing

We study the quantitative mechanisms of alternative mRNA splicing in human cells, including the readout of pre-mRNA sequence by snRNPs and RNA-binding proteins. This work has direct clinical relevance to understanding and treating diseases like Spinal Muscular Atrophy and cancer, and informs the development of splice-modifying RNA therapeutics.

Machine Learning for Genomics

We develop methods for analyzing data from MPRAs and other multiplex assays of variant effect (MAVEs). A key focus is bridging the divide between "black-box" deep neural network models in genomics and mechanistically interpretable biophysical models of gene regulation. We use surrogate models and interpretable neural networks to extract meaningful biological insights.

Quantitative Sequence-Function Relationships

Our theoretical work develops rigorous mathematical frameworks for understanding quantitative sequence-function relationships. This includes applying concepts from information theory, statistical physics, and gauge theory to extract biophysically meaningful models from experimental data and to address fundamental questions about the interpretability and identifiability of such models.

Bacterial Transcriptional Regulation

We study the biophysics of transcriptional regulation in bacteria, using precision measurements to characterize cis-regulatory energetics in living cells. This work provides insights into the fundamental mechanisms by which gene regulatory sequences control gene expression and serves as a model system for developing quantitative approaches.

Software

A Python package for creating publication-quality sequence logos. Logomaker provides flexible and customizable visualizations for DNA, RNA, and protein sequence motifs.

GitHub Repository

MAVE-NN

A Python package for learning genotype-phenotype maps from multiplex assays of variant effect. MAVE-NN uses neural networks to model complex sequence-function relationships from MPRA and other high-throughput data.

GitHub Repository

GaugeFixer

Software for overcoming parameter non-identifiability in models of sequence-function relationships. GaugeFixer addresses gauge freedoms that arise in biophysical models of gene regulation.

GitHub Repository

SUFTware

A fast and lightweight Python implementation of Bayesian Field Theory algorithms for low-dimensional statistical inference. SUFTware currently supports the one-dimenstional density estimation algorithm DEFT.
